# Research Depth Guide

Version: 1.0.0
Last updated: 2026-06-15

## Depth Levels

| Level | Use For | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Quick check | Low-risk factual lookups or orientation. | One or two reliable sources. |
| Standard research | Tool choices, technical decisions, market comparisons, implementation plans. | Primary sources plus one or more independent checks. |
| Deep research | Expensive decisions, public claims, benchmarks, legal or financial adjacency, high uncertainty. | Multiple primary sources, date checks, conflict tracking, and explicit confidence. |
| Council research | Hard synthesis with competing interpretations. | Deep research evidence packet plus model-council review. |

## Source Plan

Start with the sources most likely to be authoritative:

- official docs or changelogs for product behavior
- source repositories and release notes for software behavior
- papers and datasets for benchmarks
- laws, regulations, or government pages for policy claims
- direct CLI or API read-backs for live system state

Use search engines to find sources, not as the source of truth.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and disclose uncertainty when:

- primary sources conflict
- a current-state fact cannot be verified
- the only available evidence is marketing copy
- benchmark methods are not disclosed
- access or auth prevents direct inspection

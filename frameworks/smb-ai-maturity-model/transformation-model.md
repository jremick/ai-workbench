# AI Transformation Model

Version: 0.1.0
Last updated: 2026-06-04

## Purpose

The maturity model answers: `Where are we?`

The transformation model answers: `What needs to change for AI to become a real operating capability?`

Use this as a companion lens after the maturity assessment. The five maturity levels describe progression. The four transformation capabilities explain the kind of change required.

## The Four Transformation Capabilities

| Capability | Question It Answers | Low-Maturity Pattern | High-Maturity Pattern |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| AI Working Skill | Can people use AI effectively in real work? | AI is a personal tool used unevenly. | People can delegate, verify, improve, and teach AI-assisted work. |
| Work Design Depth | Are people redesigning work at the right level? | People prompt for tasks but do not redesign the work. | People reason about outcomes, trade-offs, systems, and handoffs. |
| Reliable AI Operations | Can AI-assisted work run safely and reliably? | Quality, risk, cost, and review are informal. | AI workflows have evidence, guardrails, measurement, and escalation paths. |
| Organizational Adaptability | Can the organization adapt as technology changes? | AI practices are one-off pilots, and roles or processes stay fixed. | Teams update workflows, roles, context, governance, and investment choices as technology shifts. |

## How This Connects To The Maturity Model

| Maturity Level | AI Working Skill | Work Design Depth | Reliable Operations | Organizational Adaptability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 - Exploring | Individual awareness | Concrete task use | Minimal controls | Change handled case by case |
| 2 - Assisted | Routine human-reviewed use | Repeatable prompting | Basic usage rules | Shared examples and lessons |
| 3 - Embedded | Workflow-level capability | Workflow redesign | Review gates and basic measures | Regular workflow retrospectives |
| 4 - Orchestrated | Agent/workflow supervision | System design | Audit trails, escalation, cost controls | Role, process, and governance updates |
| 5 - AI-native | Capability transfer and coaching | Adaptive system thinking | Managed operating metrics | Technology change absorbed into normal planning |

## How To Use It

After completing the maturity assessment, identify which transformation capability is weakest:

- If **AI Working Skill** is weakest, invest in practice, task decomposition, output review, and role-specific examples.
- If **Work Design Depth** is weakest, stop adding prompts and redesign the workflow around outcomes, trade-offs, and handoffs.
- If **Reliable AI Operations** is weakest, add review gates, evidence, measurement, safety boundaries, and escalation paths.
- If **Organizational Adaptability** is weakest, add a review cadence for roles, workflows, context, governance, performance, and technology-change decisions.

## Interpretation Rule

The weakest transformation capability explains the next investment better than the average maturity score.

Examples:

- High working skill with low reliable operations means people are moving fast without enough quality or risk control.
- High reliable operations with low working skill means governance exists, but people cannot use AI well enough to create value.
- High working skill with low work design depth means AI is improving task speed but not changing the work.
- High workflow maturity with low organizational adaptability means the first version works, but the organization will struggle to adjust as technology changes.

## SMB Guidance

Most SMBs should aim for:

- **AI Working Skill:** broad Level 2, targeted Level 3 in priority workflows.
- **Work Design Depth:** Level 3 for leaders and workflow owners, even if the broader team is Level 2.
- **Reliable AI Operations:** at least Level 3 before customer-facing or autonomous workflows.
- **Organizational Adaptability:** enough cadence to update workflows, roles, context, and controls as technology changes, not an enterprise transformation office.

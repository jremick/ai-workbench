# Sample Assessment

Version: 0.1.0
Last updated: 2026-06-04

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real company.

## Scope

Company: Summit Services, a fictional 85-person B2B services company.

Workflow assessed: customer support intake and first response.

Target: move from individual AI use to a repeatable AI-assisted support workflow.

## Evidence Reviewed

- support agents use a shared chat tool for ticket summaries and draft replies
- three agents created their own prompt examples
- no documented review checklist exists
- support knowledge articles are inconsistent and not packaged for AI use
- managers track ticket volume and response time, but not AI-assisted quality
- AI output is always reviewed before being sent to customers

## Cross-Cutting Spine Scores

| Spine | Score | Evidence | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| People & Accountability | 2 | Agents can prompt and review basic replies, but ownership and task decomposition are inconsistent. | Medium |
| Governance & Evidence | 2 | Human review is expected, but quality checks and measures are informal. | Medium |

## Transformation Profile

| Capability | Score | Evidence | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| AI Working Skill | 2 | Agents use AI routinely for summaries and draft replies. | The team has basic working skill but needs shared practice. |
| Work Design Depth | 2 | Work is still framed as faster ticket drafting, not workflow redesign. | The team has not yet redesigned intake around AI-assisted handoffs. |
| Reliable AI Operations | 2 | Human review exists, but quality checks and measures are informal. | Controls are enough for assistance, not enough for orchestration. |
| Organizational Adaptability | 1 | No review cadence exists for improving prompts, context, roles, or outcomes. | The organization has not yet built a habit of adapting the workflow as AI capability changes. |

## Assessment

Assessed working level: **Level 2 - Assisted**.

The team has moved beyond exploration because agents use AI routinely and customer-facing output receives human review. It is not yet Level 3 because the workflow, context, and quality checks are not repeatable.

Dimension level: **2**.

Spine cap: **2**.

Binding constraint: **Data & Context**.

Transformation constraint: **Organizational Adaptability**.

The biggest blocker is not tool access. The support team needs a reliable context pack: approved knowledge articles, escalation rules, tone examples, and response-quality criteria.

## Next Level Conditions

To reach Level 3 for this workflow, Summit Services should be able to show:

- a documented support intake workflow that includes AI steps
- a reusable context pack for common support categories
- a response review checklist
- examples of accepted and rejected AI-assisted replies
- basic measures for response time, rework, escalation, and customer satisfaction

## Recommended Next Three Actions

1. Build a support context pack for the top 10 ticket categories.
2. Create a one-page review checklist for AI-drafted customer replies.
3. Run a 30-day pilot comparing response time, edit rate, and escalation rate before and after the workflow change.

## What Not To Do Yet

Do not build an autonomous support agent yet. The team does not have enough context quality, measurement, or governance maturity for Level 4 orchestration.

# Model

Version: 0.1.0
Last updated: 2026-06-04

## Purpose

This model helps SMBs assess AI maturity at the level where maturity matters most: a team, function, or workflow.

Company-wide maturity claims are usually too broad to be useful. A support workflow may be embedded while finance remains assisted. A software delivery team may be orchestrated for coding work but only exploring AI for planning. Assess the specific area you want to improve.

Use [transformation-model.md](transformation-model.md) after this model when you need to understand the broader change pattern across AI working skill, work design depth, reliable AI operations, and organizational adaptability.

## Principles

1. **Evidence over optimism.** Score what people actually do, not what they intend to do.
2. **Workflow over tool access.** Buying tools is not the same as changing how work happens.
3. **Human judgment stays visible.** As maturity rises, human work shifts toward direction, review, exception handling, and system improvement.
4. **Enablement must match ambition.** Teams need time, permissions, training, data context, and support to move up a level.
5. **Selective maturity is fine.** Most SMBs should not try to make every workflow AI-native. Start where AI changes speed, quality, capacity, or customer value.

## The Five Levels

| Level | Name | What It Looks Like | Evidence Signals | Typical Next Move |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Exploring | A few people try AI for individual tasks. There is no shared standard or repeatable workflow. | Ad hoc prompts, inconsistent use, no common review practice, no measurement. | Pick approved tools, define safe use cases, and teach basic output review. |
| 2 | Assisted | People use AI routinely for drafts, summaries, research, analysis, and support tasks. Human review is expected. | Common tools, shared examples, repeat usage, visible human review. | Move from personal use to documented workflow steps. |
| 3 | Embedded | AI is built into priority workflows as a repeatable step. Teams know when to use AI and how to check results. | Documented workflow, reusable prompts or context, quality checks, basic metrics. | Connect tools and data sources for a narrow high-value process. |
| 4 | Orchestrated | AI handles connected multi-step work across systems with clear approval gates and escalation rules. | Workflow automation, agent or tool chaining, audit trail, role-specific review gates. | Add monitoring, cost controls, incident handling, and continuous improvement. |
| 5 | AI-native | Selected functions are designed AI-first. Humans direct the system, govern quality, manage exceptions, and improve performance. | AI-first process design, managed operating metrics, governance cadence, systematic improvement. | Scale only where business value and risk controls justify it. |

## The Four Assessment Dimensions

| Dimension | Question It Answers | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Direction & Use Cases | Are the right AI opportunities being chosen and prioritised? | SMBs cannot afford scattered AI activity. Maturity starts with clear, valuable use cases. |
| Workflow & Adoption | Is AI part of real work, or only individual experimentation? | Maturity shows up in repeatable workflows, not tool ownership. |
| Data & Context | Does AI have the information and constraints needed to be useful? | Generic context produces generic or unreliable outputs. |
| Tools & Integration | Are tools connected to the workflow and systems where work happens? | Teams stall when AI sits outside the operating flow. |

## The Two Cross-Cutting Spines

Spines are not separate workstreams. They cut across every dimension and cap the maturity level if they are weak.

| Spine | Question It Answers | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People & Accountability | Can people delegate, verify, own, and improve AI-assisted work? | Poor judgment turns faster output into faster mistakes. Clear ownership keeps AI from becoming everyone and no one's responsibility. |
| Governance & Evidence | Are risk, review, value, cost, and quality managed visibly? | Higher maturity increases the need for clear boundaries, evidence, auditability, and measured value. |

Use the four dimensions to see where AI is maturing. Use the two spines to decide whether that maturity is supportable.

## What The Levels Do Not Mean

- **Level 1 is not failure.** It is a normal starting point.
- **Level 2 is not enough for repeated high-value workflows.** It improves personal speed but does not usually change operating capacity.
- **Level 3 is the practical SMB target for many functions.** It gives repeatability without requiring agent operations.
- **Level 4 should be selective.** Use it for workflows with enough volume, value, and control surface to justify orchestration.
- **Level 5 is not the default goal.** It is appropriate only when AI-native operation is part of the company or function's real advantage.

## Recommended SMB Target

For most SMBs:

- Company baseline: Level 2 across staff who do knowledge work.
- Critical workflows: Level 3 for the few processes that affect customer experience, revenue, delivery speed, or operating cost.
- Selective automation: Level 4 for one or two high-volume workflows with clear review gates.
- Strategic edge: Level 5 only where the function is central to the business model and the control environment is mature.
